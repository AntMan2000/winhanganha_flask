Who we are

The First Nations Winhanganha Archive is a digital preservation initiative developed in partnership with Indigenous communities, academics and library professionals across Australia.

Our mission is to digitise, ethically manage and provide access to Indigenous cultural materials — while ensuring that Indigenous communities retain full sovereignty over their knowledge, stories and heritage.

All materials in this archive remain connected to the communities, families and knowledge holders from which they come. Access is managed in accordance with community-defined cultural protocols.

Our guiding principles

This archive is designed in accordance with the CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance (Global Indigenous Data Alliance, 2019).

Collective benefit

Data ecosystems must enable Indigenous peoples to benefit from the data and knowledge generated from and about their communities.

Authority to control

Indigenous peoples' rights and interests in Indigenous data must be recognised, and their authority to control such data must be upheld.

Responsibility

Those working with Indigenous data have a responsibility to share how those data are used to support Indigenous peoples' self-determination and collective benefit.

Ethics

Indigenous peoples' rights and wellbeing should be the primary concern at all stages of the data life cycle and across the data ecosystem.

Our partners

This initiative was developed in collaboration with Indigenous community elders, authorised delegates, community reviewers and library staff. We are grateful to all who have contributed their knowledge and guidance to this project.
